TREES FOR LIFE

NGO Website Rebuild.

The Brief

Trees for Life is an established NFP focused on rehabilitating South Australian bushland and planting trees to offset carbon emissions.

The Problem: The website needs a little polishing and clearer, simpler user flows, as well as new functionality to increase community engagement.

The Solution: A redesigned homepage that better communicates Trees for Lifeâ€™s mission to visitors and new pages with a new workshop sign-up feature and a simplified donation process.

The Research

The User Journey helped guide any of the later design aesthetic and feature decisions. Our focus was to engage members of the community and in turn increase the numbers of TFL members, we used heuristic evaluations to increase usabillity and design of the websites homepage.

User Journey

User Flow

By identifying two key user flows we were able to improve the voerall usabillity of the site for both the users looking to donate as well as the users looking to attend workshops.

Style Tile

The idea was to maintain a similar brand styling to the original site, by using welcoming imagery, colours and text. By using the blue and green colours as the primary branding colours we were able to create a calming yet modern feel, with legible fonts ad communtiy inspired images, the final website has a community driven but clean design .

Design Iterations

Throughout the project we continued to iterate our features and design to create an aesthetic ally pleasing yet functional website with a focus on user centric design.
